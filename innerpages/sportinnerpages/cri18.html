Cricket World Cup 2023: Virat Kohli hits record 50th ODI century - stats that make him the greatest

3 days ago

Virat Kohli now stands alone in one-day international cricket.

His century in the victory over New Zealand in Wednesday's World Cup semi-final was the India superstar's 50th in the format, taking him one clear of the legendary Sachin Tendulkar. BBC Sport takes a look at the statistics that make Kohli the 50-over GOAT..

Tendulkar scored his 49th century in his penultimate ODI, meaning he got there in his 451st innings. Kohli equalled the Little Master's haul in 277 knocks and has now gone past it in 279. He has also done so at a better strike-rate - 93.62 runs per 100 balls compared to Tendulkar's 86.23 - and, helped by batting in the middle order while Tendulkar was largely an ODI opener, with a better average.

"This all feels like a dream," said Kohli after making 117 against New Zealand, with partner Anushka Sharma and Tendulkar watching in the stands at Wankhede Stadium

"My life partner, the person I love the most, she is sitting there. My hero Sachin is sitting there. I was able to get the 50th in front of them and these fans in such a historic venue. It was amazing."
