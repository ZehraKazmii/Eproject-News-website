French film director Jacques Doillon calls storm of #MeToo allegations 'lies'

French film director Jacques Doillon, swept up in a storm of #MeToo allegations by several actors including Judith Godreche, denounced on Friday the "lies" and "false accusations" against him. "That Judith Godreche and other women through her have the heart to denounce a system, an era, a society, is courageous, commendable and necessary," said the director. Godreche, 51, claimed Doillon, 79, took advantage of her while directing her in one of his films when she was 15. He was 29 years older at the time.
